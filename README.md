
### <div align="center">Representation of Turkish Phonological Terms in Generative Large Language Models</div>
#### <div align="right">Erkut RESULOĞLU</div>
### Abstract

*<p align="justify">Contemporary research has increasingly focused on the approaches and outcomes of LLMs in the context of technological advancements. Due to the nature of language, sounds must be identified, learned, and digitized. Aim of this study is to identify the phonological structures of LLMs at a terminological level. Ability of LLMs to identify vowels, consonants, and phonetic structures is evaluated using benchmark datasets. The study employed three different benchmark datasets. Benchmark datasets incorporate both open-ended and multiple-choice question formats. Phonological terms were obtained through responses from cloud-based LLMs within the Ollama workspace. Findings of the study suggest that LLMs exhibit considerable terminological diversity. This aligns with the observed of LLMs to focus on detail and micro-terminology in their definitions. In conclusion, the study demonstrates that LLMs possess the ability to analyse language; however, they are unable to provide either internal or systematic consistency in terminological standardization.</p>*

**Keywords:** *Large language models (LLMs), generative artificial intelligence, Turkish phonology, text-based models, terminology.*
